# Wikipedia: Stuart J. Russell
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuart_J._Russell
Fetched: 2026-05-07

## Biographical (verbatim)
"Stuart Jonathan Russell" — "born 1962" in "Portsmouth, England."
- BA first-class honours, Oxford physics, 1982
- PhD computer science, Stanford, 1986

## UC Berkeley (verbatim)
"A professor of computer science at the University of California, Berkeley"
"Holds the Smith-Zadeh Chair in Engineering"
"Founded and directs the Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence (CHAI) at UC Berkeley"

## "Human Compatible" (2019, verbatim)
Subtitle: "Artificial Intelligence and the Problem of Control"
Focus: ensuring AI systems "align with human values and remain controllable"

## AI safety (verbatim)
Signed the FLI 2023 open letter calling for a pause on systems more powerful than GPT-4.
Russell warned: "the AGI race is a race towards the edge of a cliff."

## Russell & Norvig textbook (verbatim)
With Peter Norvig: "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach"
Used in "more than 1,500 universities in 135 countries."
