# Wikipedia: Artificial Intelligence Act
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence_Act
Fetched: 2026-04-30

## Official name (verbatim)
"Artificial Intelligence Act" (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
Entered into force: 2024-08-01

## Risk classification (verbatim)
- **Unacceptable risk** — "AI applications in this category are banned"
- **High-risk** — Systems "expected to pose significant threats to health, safety, or the fundamental rights of persons"
- **Limited risk** — Systems with "transparency obligations"
- **Minimal risk** — "Most AI applications are expected to fall into this category. These systems are not regulated"

## GPAI rules (verbatim)
"Open source models, developers must publish a training data summary and a copyright policy; closed-source models must meet broader transparency requirements."
High-capability threshold: "more than 10^25 floating-point operations to train" → "extra evaluation."

## Penalties (verbatim)
- Unacceptable risk: "up to EUR 35,000,000 or...up to 7% of its total worldwide annual turnover"
- Other operator obligations: "up to EUR 15,000,000 or 3% of worldwide annual turnover"
- Misinformation: "up to EUR 7,500,000 or 1% of worldwide annual turnover"

## Phased enforcement (verbatim)
- 6 months: bans on unacceptable risk
- 9 months: codes of practice
- 12 months: general-purpose AI systems
- 24 months: most other obligations
- 36 months: some high-risk obligations
