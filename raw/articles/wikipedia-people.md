## Sam Altman
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Altman

- Born: 1985-04-22 in Chicago
- Current role: CEO of OpenAI
- Education: Stanford (two years, did not complete degree)
- OpenAI: co-founded 2015; CEO since 2019
- Removed by board: 2023-11-17 ("was not consistently candid in his communications")
- Reinstated: 2023-11-21
- Y Combinator: joined 2011 (partner, part-time); president from February 2014; relationship terminated early 2020
- Other ventures: Worldcoin (co-founded Tools For Humanity, 2019); Helion Energy (chairman); Hydrazine Capital (co-founded with brother Jack, 2012); Reddit CEO for 8 days in 2014, board member until 2022

## Dario Amodei
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dario_Amodei

- Born: 1983 in San Francisco
- Current role: CEO of Anthropic
- Education: Stanford (BS Physics); Princeton (MS, PhD Biophysics)
- OpenAI: VP of Research starting 2016; departed due to "directional differences"
- Founded Anthropic: 2021 with sister Daniela and other former OpenAI senior members
- Essay "Machines of Loving Grace": October 2024
  - Quote: "I think that most people are underestimating just how radical the upside of AI could be, just as I think most people are underestimating how bad the risks could be."
- Policy: advocates "entente" strategy where democracies use advanced AI for military advantage while sharing benefits among cooperating nations

## Geoffrey Hinton
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton

- Born: 1947-12-06 in London
- Education: King's College Cambridge (BA experimental psychology, 1970); Edinburgh (PhD AI, 1978)
- Currently: University Professor Emeritus, University of Toronto (since 1987)
- 2018 Turing Award (verbatim): "for conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing." Joint with Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun.
- 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics (verbatim): jointly with John Hopfield "for foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks." Boltzmann machine cited.
- Google departure (May 2023, verbatim): "I left so that I could talk about the dangers of AI without considering how this impacts Google."
- AI risk: "wipe out humanity" — "not inconceivable"; chatbots "can learn independently and share knowledge" and "whenever one copy acquires new information, it is automatically disseminated."

## Yann LeCun
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun

- Born: 1960-07-08 in Soisy-sous-Montmorency, France
- Education: ESIEE Paris (Diplôme d'Ingénieur, 1983); Pierre and Marie Curie University (PhD, 1987)
- Current: "Jacob T. Schwartz Professor of Computer Science at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences at New York University" and Executive Chair of Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (as of November 2025)
- Meta/FAIR: joined Facebook 2013 as chief AI scientist; led FAIR; left 2025-11-19
- 2018 Turing Award (verbatim): "In 2018, LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, and Geoffrey Hinton received the Turing Award from the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) for their work on deep learning."
- CNN work (verbatim): At Bell Labs, "developed a number of new machine learning methods, such as a biologically inspired model of image recognition called convolutional neural networks (LeNet), applied to handwriting recognition and optical character recognition."
- 2025 quote: "I'm sure there's a lot of people at Meta who would like me to NOT tell the world that LLMs basically are a dead end when it comes to superintelligence."

## Ilya Sutskever
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Sutskever

- Born: 1986 in Nizhny Novgorod, Soviet Union
- Education: PhD computer science, University of Toronto (2013); doctoral advisor Geoffrey Hinton
- OpenAI co-founder (verbatim): "At the end of 2015, Sutskever left Google to become cofounder and chief scientist of the newly founded organization OpenAI."
- November 2023 board vote (verbatim): "The board fired Sam Altman, saying that 'he was not consistently candid in his communications with the board'." Sutskever participated; later expressed regret.
- Departure (verbatim): "In May 2024, Sutskever announced his departure from OpenAI to focus on a new project that was 'very personally meaningful' to him."
- Safe Superintelligence Inc. (verbatim): "In June 2024, Sutskever announced Safe Superintelligence Inc., a new company he founded with Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy." Mission: "the new company's 'first product will be the safe superintelligence, and it will not do anything else up until then'."

## Demis Hassabis
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis

- Born: 1976-07-27 in London
- Background: chess master (Elo 2300 at 13); video-game AI at Bullfrog Productions and Lionhead Studios
- Education: Queens' College, Cambridge (Computer Science, double first, 1997); UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology (PhD cognitive neuroscience, 2009)
- DeepMind: co-founded 2010 in London with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman
- Google acquisition (verbatim): "In 2014, Google purchased DeepMind for £400 million."
- 2024 Nobel Prize Chemistry (verbatim): Hassabis and John M. Jumper "jointly awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for their AI research contributions for protein structure prediction."
- Current role: CEO and co-founder of Google DeepMind (post-merger)
