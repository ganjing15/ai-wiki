# EU Artificial Intelligence Act

## Source
[Wikipedia: Artificial Intelligence Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence_Act) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-eu-ai-act.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-eu-ai-act.md).

## Identification (verbatim, [EUR-Lex CELEX:32024R1689](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689))
Raw: [eur-lex-ai-act.md](../raw/articles/eur-lex-ai-act.md).

- Official title: "Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 June 2024 laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence"
- Published in the Official Journal: **12.7.2024** (OJ L, 2024/1689)
- Entered into force: **2024-08-01** per Wikipedia
- Article 113 specifies a phased implementation timeline rather than a single date

## Risk classification (verbatim)
- **Unacceptable risk** — "AI applications in this category are banned"
- **High-risk** — Systems "expected to pose significant threats to health, safety, or the fundamental rights of persons"
- **Limited risk** — Systems with "transparency obligations"
- **Minimal risk** — "Most AI applications are expected to fall into this category. These systems are not regulated"

## General-purpose AI rules (verbatim)
> "Open source models, developers must publish a training data summary and a copyright policy; closed-source models must meet broader transparency requirements."

High-capability threshold: models requiring "more than 10²⁵ floating-point operations to train" must "undergo extra evaluation."

## Penalties (verbatim)
- Unacceptable risk violations: "up to EUR 35,000,000 or...up to 7% of its total worldwide annual turnover"
- Other operator obligations: "up to EUR 15,000,000 or 3% of worldwide annual turnover"
- Misinformation: "up to EUR 7,500,000 or 1% of worldwide annual turnover"

## Phased enforcement (verbatim)
- 6 months: bans on "unacceptable risk" AI systems
- 9 months: codes of practice
- 12 months: general-purpose AI systems
- 24 months: most other obligations
- 36 months: some high-risk AI system obligations
