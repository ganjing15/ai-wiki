# US Executive Order 14110

## Source
[Wikipedia: Executive Order 14110](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Order_14110) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-us-eo-14110.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-us-eo-14110.md).

## Identification (verbatim)
- Full name: "Executive Order on Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy Development and Use of Artificial Intelligence"
- Signed: **2023-10-30** by **President Joe Biden**

## Required actions (verbatim from Wikipedia)
- Federal agencies create chief AI officer positions
- Department of Commerce's NIST: develop generative AI resources
- Department of Homeland Security: AI security guidelines and critical infrastructure coordination
- General Services Administration, Department of Education, National Science Foundation: appointed chief AI officers
- Department of Veterans Affairs: AI technology competition for healthcare worker burnout

## Revocation (verbatim)
> "Hours after his inauguration as the 47th president of the United States, Donald Trump rescinded the order" on **2025-01-20**.

## 10²⁶ FLOPs reporting threshold (verbatim from secondary sources)
Raw: [eo-14110-details.md](../raw/articles/eo-14110-details.md). Sourced from a WebSearch summary of the Federal Register and law-firm summaries (the federalregister.gov direct fetch was redirected/blocked).

> "Executive Order 14110 ... specifies 10^26 FLOPs as the threshold for triggering certain reporting obligations to the Federal Government (Section 4.2(b)(i))."

Definition of dual-use foundation model (verbatim, summarized from EO text):
> "Trained on broad data; generally uses self-supervision; contains at least tens of billions of parameters; is applicable across a wide range of contexts; and that exhibits, or could be easily modified to exhibit, high levels of performance at tasks that pose a serious risk to security, national economic security, national public health or safety, or any combination of those matters."

Section 4.2(a)(i) reporting requirements (per same summary): training/development info; ownership and possession of model weights and physical/cybersecurity measures; results of red-team testing per US AI Safety Institute guidance.

> Note: This material has not been verified against the Federal Register PDF directly. Treat the threshold and section numbers as well-attested by multiple secondary sources but flag for primary-source verification when access is available.

## Trump-administration successor: America's AI Action Plan (2025-07-23)
Source: [whitehouse.gov press release](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2025/07/white-house-unveils-americas-ai-action-plan/) and PDF [Americas-AI-Action-Plan.pdf](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Americas-AI-Action-Plan.pdf). Raw: [america-ai-action-plan.md](../raw/articles/america-ai-action-plan.md).

- Title (verbatim): **"Winning the AI Race: America's AI Action Plan"**
- Date: **2025-07-23**
- Scope: "over 90 federal policy actions"
- **Three pillars (verbatim)**:
  1. Accelerating Innovation
  2. Building American AI Infrastructure
  3. Leading in International Diplomacy and Security
- Cross-cutting priorities (verbatim summary): exporting American AI to allies, expediting data center permits, removing regulatory barriers, ensuring frontier models remain "objective and free from ideological bias."
- Quotes:
  > **Marco Rubio** (Secretary of State): "Winning the AI Race is non-negotiable. America must continue to be the dominant force in artificial intelligence."
  > **Michael Kratsios** (OSTP Director): "This plan galvanizes Federal efforts to turbocharge our innovation capacity, build cutting-edge infrastructure, and lead globally."

Trump signed three accompanying executive orders alongside the plan; specific titles/numbers should be fetched from federalregister.gov when accessible.
