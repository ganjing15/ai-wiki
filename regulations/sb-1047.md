# SB 1047 — Safe and Secure Innovation for Frontier Artificial Intelligence Models Act

## Source
[Wikipedia: SB 1047](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SB_1047) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-sb-1047.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-sb-1047.md).

## Identification (verbatim)
- Full name: **"Safe and Secure Innovation for Frontier Artificial Intelligence Models Act"**
- Author: California State Senator Scott Wiener
- Introduced: **2024-02-07**

## Coverage thresholds (verbatim)
- "Trained using a quantity of computing power greater than 10²⁶ integer or floating-point operations"
- "Cost more than $100 million to train"
- Fine-tuned models using over $10 million also covered

## Newsom veto
- Date: **2024-09-29**
- Rationale (verbatim): the regulatory framework was "based only on computational size" without considering "whether systems operated in high-risk environments."

## Lab responses (verbatim)
- **Anthropic**: Dario Amodei said the amended version was "substantially improved" and believed "its benefits likely outweigh its costs"
- **OpenAI**: opposed; claimed it would "hurt innovation"
- **Google, Microsoft, Anthropic** all "proposed substantial amendments"

## Significance
The 10²⁶ FLOPs threshold mirrors the threshold in [Biden's EO 14110](us-executive-order-ai.md). SB 1047 was the most prominent attempt at US state-level AI safety regulation; its September 2024 veto effectively foreclosed state-level frontier-model regulation in the US for the immediate term. The California legislative debate is widely cited in subsequent EU AI Act / AI Action Plan policy discussions.
