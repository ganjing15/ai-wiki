# China AI Regulation

## Source
[Wikipedia: Regulation of artificial intelligence § China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation_of_artificial_intelligence#China) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-china-ai-regulation.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-china-ai-regulation.md).

## August 2023 Generative AI measures (verbatim)
> "On August 15, 2023, China's first generative AI measures officially came into force, becoming one of the first comprehensive national regulatory frameworks for generative AI."

The measures apply broadly to all providers offering generative AI services to Chinese citizens, including foreign companies, and establish requirements for "data protection, transparency, and algorithmic accountability."

## 2025 Synthetic content labeling (verbatim)
> "In 2025, Chinese regulators issued the Measures for Labeling of AI-Generated Synthetic Content."

Mandates disclosure labels and embedded metadata for AI-generated materials. Online service providers must reveal identity and detect synthetic content with "conspicuous consumer disclosures."

## Earlier regulations
- **Algorithm Recommendation Provisions** (March 2022)
- **Deep Synthesis Provisions** (January 2023)

Both shape governance of "AI-driven systems, including requirements for watermarking and algorithm filing."

## Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC) — verbatim
> "As of mid-2024, over 1,400 AI algorithms had been already registered under the CAC's algorithm filing regime."
