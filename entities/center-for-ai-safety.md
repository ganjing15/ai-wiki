# Center for AI Safety (CAIS)

## Source
[Wikipedia: Center for AI Safety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_for_AI_Safety) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-center-for-ai-safety.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-center-for-ai-safety.md).

## Founding (verbatim)
> "It was founded in 2022 by Dan Hendrycks and Oliver Zhang."

Headquarters: San Francisco, California.

## May 2023 Statement on AI Risk
The full 22-word statement (verbatim, sourced via [Wikipedia: Existential risk from AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existential_risk_from_artificial_intelligence)):

> "Mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war."

Per CAIS's own Wikipedia entry:
> "In May 2023, CAIS published the statement on AI risk of extinction signed by hundreds of professors of AI, leaders of major AI companies, and other public figures."

Notable signatories cited: **Dario Amodei** (Anthropic), **Sam Altman** (OpenAI), and "other AI industry leaders." (Geoffrey Hinton was also a signatory, per his own Wikipedia page.)

## Outputs (verbatim)
- "An Overview of Catastrophic AI Risks" — autonomous warfare, pandemic engineering, AI deception/hacking
- Joint with Carnegie Mellon: "an automated way to discover adversarial attacks of large language models" (universal adversarial suffixes)
- "Intro to ML Safety" online course
- A compute cluster for AI safety research
- A fellowship program for philosophy professors
