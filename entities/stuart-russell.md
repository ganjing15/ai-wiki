# Stuart J. Russell

## Source
[Wikipedia: Stuart J. Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuart_J._Russell) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-stuart-russell.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-stuart-russell.md).

## Biographical (verbatim)
> "Stuart Jonathan Russell" — "born 1962" in "Portsmouth, England."

Education: BA first-class honours, Oxford physics (1982); PhD computer science, Stanford (1986).

## UC Berkeley (verbatim)
> "A professor of computer science at the University of California, Berkeley."
> "Holds the Smith-Zadeh Chair in Engineering."
> "Founded and directs the Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence (CHAI) at UC Berkeley."

## "Human Compatible" (2019, verbatim)
Subtitle: "Artificial Intelligence and the Problem of Control."
Focus: ensuring AI systems "align with human values and remain controllable."

## AI safety stance (verbatim)
- Signed the FLI 2023 Pause Letter calling for a pause on systems more powerful than GPT-4
- Cited as a signatory of the May 2023 CAIS Statement on AI Risk
- Quote: "the AGI race is a race towards the edge of a cliff."

## Russell & Norvig textbook (verbatim)
With Peter Norvig: "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach"

> Used in "more than 1,500 universities in 135 countries."

## Significance
Together with Yoshua Bengio (LawZero), Russell (CHAI) leads the most prominent academic AI-safety institutional effort. The Russell & Norvig textbook's 1,500-university adoption makes it the most widely used AI textbook in the world — a meaningful institutional anchor.
