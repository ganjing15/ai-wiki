# Nvidia (AI / data-center business)

## Source
[Wikipedia: Nvidia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-nvidia.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-nvidia.md).

## Leadership
**Jensen Huang** — "founder, president and chief executive officer."

## Revenue trajectory (verbatim)
| Fiscal year | Revenue |
|-------------|---------|
| FY2024 | $60.9B |
| FY2025 | $130B |
| FY2026 | $215.9B |

FY2025 segment split: Compute & networking **$116.2B (89.0%)**, Graphics **$14.3B (11.0%)** — the AI-driven inversion of Nvidia's historical gaming-dominated mix is now plain.

## Blackwell architecture (verbatim)
> "On March 18, Nvidia announced its new AI chip and microarchitecture Blackwell, named after mathematician David Blackwell."

By November 2024: "the entire 2025 production of all Nvidia's Blackwell chips was already sold out" (per Morgan Stanley).

## Market-cap milestones (verbatim)
- **2024-06-18**: $3 trillion ("the world's most valuable")
- **2025-07-10**: $4 trillion ("first company to reach a market cap of $4 trillion")
- **2025-10-29**: $5 trillion ("first company to reach a market capitalization of $5 trillion")

## DeepSeek-driven drop (verbatim)
> "In January 2025, Nvidia saw the largest one-day loss in market capitalization for a U.S. company in history at $600 billion. This was due to DeepSeek."

(Cross-ref: [wikipedia-deepseek.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-deepseek.md) confirms 2025-01-27 as the day DeepSeek-R1 surpassed ChatGPT on the iOS App Store.)

## Significance
Nvidia is effectively the AI economy's hardware spine — the FY2024 → FY2026 revenue jump from $60.9B to $215.9B (3.5×) and the $3T → $5T market-cap arc in 16 months are the clearest single quantitative evidence of the post-ChatGPT capex boom.
