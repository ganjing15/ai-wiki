# Yoshua Bengio

## Source
[Wikipedia: Yoshua Bengio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshua_Bengio) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-yoshua-bengio.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-yoshua-bengio.md).

## Biographical (verbatim)
> "Born March 5, 1964" in Paris, France.

Education: McGill University — BSc (electrical engineering), MSc and PhD (computer science).

## 2018 Turing Award (verbatim)
> "Bengio received the 2018 ACM A.M. Turing Award" alongside [[Geoffrey Hinton]] and [[Yann LeCun]] "for their foundational work on deep learning."

## Mila (verbatim)
> "Founded Mila, the Quebec Artificial Intelligence (AI) Institute, and was its scientific director until 2025."

## International AI Safety Report (verbatim)
- Announcement: November 2023 AI Safety Summit (Bletchley) — Bengio to "lead an international scientific report on the safety of advanced AI"
- Interim report: AI Seoul Summit, May 2024
- Full report: "January 2025 as the International AI Safety Report"

## LawZero (verbatim)
> "In June 2025, Bengio had launched a nonprofit organization, LawZero, aimed at building 'honest' AI systems."

> "The group [is] developing a system called Scientist AI, intended to act as a guardrail."

## Significance
Bengio is the third Turing Award co-winner (alongside Hinton and LeCun); together they share the foundational deep-learning credit. Notably, **Bengio and Hinton are publicly aligned on AI risk concerns** while **LeCun is the prominent skeptic** — see [[AGI Risk]]. Bengio's role leading the **International AI Safety Report** (commissioned at Bletchley, completed Jan 2025) makes him the most institutionally-recognized scientific voice in formal AI-risk policy.
