# Demis Hassabis

## Source
[Wikipedia: Demis Hassabis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-people.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-people.md).

## Biographical
- Born: 1976-07-27 in London
- Education: Queens' College, Cambridge (Computer Science, double first, 1997); UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology (PhD cognitive neuroscience, 2009)
- Background: chess master (Elo 2300 at age 13); video-game AI at Bullfrog Productions and Lionhead Studios

## DeepMind founding
- Co-founded **DeepMind in London in 2010** with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman.
- Google acquisition (verbatim): "In 2014, Google purchased DeepMind for £400 million."
  (Note: prior version of this page said "~$500M". Wikipedia source says **£400 million**.)

## Current role
CEO and co-founder of Google DeepMind (post-merger in April 2023; see [[Google DeepMind]] for merger details).

## 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry (verbatim, [Wikipedia: AlphaFold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaFold))
> "Demis Hassabis and John Jumper shared one half of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry, awarded 'for protein structure prediction,' while the other half went to David Baker 'for computational protein design.'"

## AlphaGo (verbatim, [Wikipedia: AlphaGo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo))
Raw: [wikipedia-alphago.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-alphago.md).

> "AlphaGo is a computer program that plays the board game Go. It was developed by the London-based DeepMind Technologies, an acquired subsidiary of Google."

**Lee Sedol match (2016):** dates "9, 10, 12, 13, and 15 March 2016", Four Seasons Hotel in Seoul. AlphaGo won 4 games, Lee Sedol won 1.

**Ke Jie match (2017):** Future of Go Summit, Wuzhen, May 2017. "Master won all three games against Ke Jie." Prize "1.5 million dollar."
