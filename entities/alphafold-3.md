## AlphaFold 3 capabilities (verbatim)
Predicts structures of "protein complexes with DNA, RNA, post-translational modifications and selected ligands and ions."

## *Nature* publication (verbatim, AlphaFold 2)
> "On 15 July 2021, the AlphaFold 2 paper was published in *Nature* as an advance access publication alongside open source software and a searchable database of species proteomes."

### Nature paper (Abramson et al., 2024-05-08, [Nature 630:493–500](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07487-w))
Raw: [alphafold-3.md](../raw/papers/alphafold-3.md). Sourced via WebSearch summary because Nature returns 303 redirect on direct fetch.

Architecture (verbatim summary):
> "Substantially updated diffusion-based architecture that is capable of predicting the joint structure of complexes including proteins, nucleic acids, small molecules, ions and modified residues."

Accuracy (verbatim summary):
> "Far greater accuracy for protein–ligand interactions compared with state-of-the-art docking tools, much higher accuracy for protein–nucleic acid interactions compared with nucleic-acid-specific predictors and substantially higher antibody–antigen prediction accuracy compared with AlphaFold-Multimer v.2.3."

> "For the interactions of proteins with other molecule types there is at least a 50% improvement compared with existing prediction methods, and for some important categories of interaction prediction accuracy has doubled."

Note: original paper shipped with errata that necessitated an Addendum ([s41586-024-08416-7](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08416-7)).

## 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry (verbatim)
> "Demis Hassabis and John Jumper shared one half of the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry, awarded 'for protein structure prediction,' while the other half went to David Baker 'for computational protein design.'"
