# Ilya Sutskever

## Source
[Wikipedia: Ilya Sutskever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Sutskever) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-people.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-people.md).

## Biographical
- Born: 1986 in Nizhny Novgorod, Soviet Union
- Education: PhD computer science, University of Toronto (2013); doctoral advisor Geoffrey Hinton

## OpenAI co-founder (verbatim)
> "At the end of 2015, Sutskever left Google to become cofounder and chief scientist of the newly founded organization OpenAI."

## November 2023 board vote (verbatim)
> "The board fired Sam Altman, saying that 'he was not consistently candid in his communications with the board'."

Sutskever participated in the decision and later expressed regret.

## Departure from OpenAI (verbatim)
> "In May 2024, Sutskever announced his departure from OpenAI to focus on a new project that was 'very personally meaningful' to him."

## Safe Superintelligence Inc. (verbatim)
> "In June 2024, Sutskever announced Safe Superintelligence Inc., a new company he founded with Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy."

> Mission: "the new company's 'first product will be the safe superintelligence, and it will not do anything else up until then'."

## AlexNet (2012) — verbatim, [Wikipedia: AlexNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlexNet)
Raw: [wikipedia-alexnet.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-alexnet.md).

> "Alex Krizhevsky in collaboration with Ilya Sutskever and his Ph.D. advisor Geoffrey Hinton at the University of Toronto"

> "The network achieved a top-5 error rate of 15.3% to win the contest, more than 10.8% above the runner-up."

Yann LeCun on AlexNet at ECCV 2012: "an unequivocal turning point in the history of computer vision."
