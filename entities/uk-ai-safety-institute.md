# UK AI Safety Institute (now AI Security Institute)

## Source
[Wikipedia: AI Safety Institute (United Kingdom)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_Safety_Institute_(United_Kingdom)) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-uk-aisi.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-uk-aisi.md).

## Establishment (verbatim from raw)
- Founded: November 2023 (evolved from Frontier AI Taskforce, April 2023)
- Parent: Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT)
- Headquarters: London; San Francisco office opened May 2024

## Name history (verbatim)
> "Frontier AI Taskforce" → "AI Safety Institute" (2023) → "AI Security Institute" (February 2025)

## Role and tooling (verbatim)
> "Open-sourced an AI safety tool called 'Inspect', which evaluates AI model capabilities such as reasoning and their degree of autonomy."

### Inspect AI Framework (primary source, verbatim)
Source: [inspect.aisi.org.uk](https://inspect.aisi.org.uk). Raw: [aisi-inspect.md](../raw/articles/aisi-inspect.md). Released May 2024.

> "An open-source framework for large language model evaluations" — "Developed by the UK AI Security Institute and Meridian Labs"

Capabilities (verbatim):
- "A collection of over 200 pre-built evaluations ready to run on any model"
- "Flexible support for tool calling—custom and MCP tools, as well as built-in bash, python, text editing, web search, web browsing, and computer tools"
- "Support for agent evaluations, including flexible built-in agents, multi-agent primitives"
- "A sandboxing system that supports running untrusted model code in Docker, Kubernetes, Modal, Proxmox"

## US partnership (verbatim)
> "Agreed with US in April 2024 to collaborate on joint safety tests."

## Counterparts cited in same Wikipedia article
- **US AISI / CAISI**: founded November 2023 within NIST; renamed "Center for AI Standards and Innovation" (CAISI) June 2025; $10M budget allocated March 2024; consortium of "more than 200 organizations such as Google, Anthropic or Microsoft"
- **Japan AISI**: founded February 2024 under Information Technology Promotion Agency; ~23 staff

### US CAISI mission — primary source (verbatim, [nist.gov/aisi](https://www.nist.gov/aisi))
Raw: [nist-caisi.md](../raw/articles/nist-caisi.md).

> CAISI "will serve as industry's primary point of contact within the U.S. government to facilitate testing and collaborative research related to harnessing and securing the potential of commercial AI systems."

Functions: developing AI security guidelines; voluntary agreements with AI developers; leading evaluations of AI capabilities and vulnerabilities; coordinating with federal agencies; representing US interests in international AI standards.
