# Yann LeCun

## Source
[Wikipedia: Yann LeCun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-people.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-people.md).

## Biographical
- Born: 1960-07-08 in Soisy-sous-Montmorency, France
- Education: ESIEE Paris (Diplôme d'Ingénieur, 1983); Pierre and Marie Curie University (PhD computer science, 1987)

## Current role (verbatim)
> "Jacob T. Schwartz Professor of Computer Science at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences at New York University" and "Executive Chair of Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (as of November 2025)."

## Meta tenure
- Joined Facebook (now Meta) in 2013 as chief AI scientist; led FAIR
- Departed Meta: **2025-11-19**

## 2018 Turing Award (verbatim)
> "In 2018, LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, and Geoffrey Hinton received the Turing Award from the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) for their work on deep learning."

## CNN work (verbatim, [Wikipedia: Yann LeCun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun))
> At Bell Labs, "developed a number of new machine learning methods, such as a biologically inspired model of image recognition called convolutional neural networks (LeNet), applied to handwriting recognition and optical character recognition."

## 2025 quote (verbatim)
> "I'm sure there's a lot of people at Meta who would like me to NOT tell the world that LLMs basically are a dead end when it comes to superintelligence."
