# Open Source AI Safety Debate

## Source
[Wikipedia: Open-source artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_artificial_intelligence) → raw excerpt at [wikipedia-open-source-ai.md](../raw/articles/wikipedia-open-source-ai.md).

## OSI definition (verbatim, OSAID 2024)
> "An AI system that is freely available to use, study, modify, and share."

Encompasses "datasets used to train the model, its code, and model parameters."

OSAID 1.0 requires:
- Full release of software for processing data and making inferences.
- For training data: "access to details about the data used to train the AI so others can understand and re-create it."

## Examples in source
- **Llama** (Meta): Llama 1 and 2 (2023); Llama 3.1 405B (2024)
- **Mistral / Mixtral** (Mistral AI, 2023)
- **DeepSeek**: V3 (December 2024), R1 (January 2025) — both under MIT license

## Safety debate (verbatim, [Wikipedia: Open-source AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_artificial_intelligence))
**Concerns**:
> "Open models have fewer ways to prevent them from being used for malicious activities."

Bioterrorism groups potentially removing safeguards.

**Benefits**:
> "Open-source models are harder to censor than close-sourced ones" and offer increased transparency for inspection and accountability.

## Proponents vs. critics (verbatim)
> "Meta champions openness; OpenAI shifted toward proprietary approaches, not releasing GPT-3 weights publicly."

## "Open-weight" vs "open-source" (verbatim)
> "**Openwashing**" — models that release only weights while withholding training data and code—appearing open but remaining "mostly closed."
