# Transformer Architecture ("Attention Is All You Need")

## Source
[arXiv:1706.03762](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) — Vaswani et al., "Attention Is All You Need" (NIPS 2017). Raw excerpt at [attention-is-all-you-need.md](../raw/papers/attention-is-all-you-need.md).

## Authors (verbatim)
> "Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones, Aidan Gomez, Łukasz Kaiser, and Illia Polosukhin"

## Publication (verbatim)
> "June 12, 2017" at "the 31st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS)"

## What it introduced (verbatim)
> "Introduced a new deep learning architecture known as the transformer, based on the attention mechanism."

Specifically named contributions:
- **Scaled dot-product attention**
- **Self-attention**
- **Multi-head attention**
- **Positional encoding**

## Citation reach (verbatim)
> "As of 2025, the paper has been cited more than 173,000 times, placing it among the top ten most-cited papers of the 21st century."

## Authors' diaspora (verbatim)
> "After the paper was published by Google, each of the authors left the company to join other companies or to found startups."

Tracked in this wiki:
- **Aidan Gomez** → co-founded [[Cohere]] (2019)
- **Noam Shazeer** → co-founded Character.AI; later returned to Google
- (Other authors went on to Adept, Anthropic, Inceptive, OpenAI, Pinterest, etc. — diaspora details not yet sourced primary)

## Significance
The Transformer is the architectural foundation for essentially every modern LLM — GPT-3/4/5, Claude, Gemini, Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek, Qwen all use Transformer or Transformer-derivative architectures. The "all 8 authors left Google" detail is now widely cited as evidence that frontier capability concentrates with researchers, not employers.
